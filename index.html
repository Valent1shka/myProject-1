  • Neptun

    Neptune is the farthest planet from our Sun, mesmerizing with its deep ultramarine color due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere. This ice giant has the fastest supersonic winds in the solar system, blowing at speeds of over two thousand kilometers per hour. In the depths of Neptune, the pressure and temperature are so extreme that carbon there turns into real diamonds, which constantly fall to the planet's core in the form of precious rains.

  • Venus

    Venus is Earth's infernal twin, holding the title of the hottest planet in our solar system. This is due to a dense layer of carbon dioxide, which creates a devastating greenhouse effect and heats the surface to four hundred and sixty degrees Celsius. Its sky constantly rains caustic sulfuric acid, and the pressure on its surface is ninety times that of Earth, making this world one of the most brutal in our cosmic neighborhood.

  • Saturn

    Saturn is known as a majestic gas giant and the recognized owner of the brightest ring system, consisting of billions of particles of ice and cosmic dust. This planet has an amazingly low density, as it is composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. Because of this feature, if there were a giant ocean in space where Saturn could be lowered, this huge striped disk would simply float on its surface like a light ball.

  • Jupiter

    Jupiter is the Earth's greatest planetary protector, weighing twice as much as all the other planets in our system combined. On its surface, the Great Red Spot rages - a giant hurricane larger than Earth and has not subsided for several centuries. Thanks to its colossal mass and powerful gravity, this giant works as a real cosmic shield, intercepting and destroying dangerous comets and asteroids.
